Perceptual Experiment on DMI Performance

⚠️ Please read this page carefully before starting. If you do not follow the instructions correctly, you will not receive compensation.

Purpose of the Experiment

This study is part of research on audience perception of **Digital Musical Instruments (DMIs)** and their performance. DMIs often allow performers to create sound through **gestures without physically touching an object**.

Our objective is to investigate a perceptual phenomenon where audiences, while watching such a performance, may strongly feel the presence of a musical instrument that is **not actually there**. We refer to this as the **"Illusion of Instrument Presence"**.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: These videos are not simple pantomime, but performances using gesture input for musical expression. To judge if the illusion occurs, please carefully observe and listen to **both the video and the sound**.

Your participation will help us understand how the **visual and auditory elements of a performance influence audience perception** and **appreciation** of these new forms of musical expression.

---

Participation Details

  • You will watch a total of eight short performance videos (approximately 10 seconds each).
  • **While watching** the video, you will be asked to draw on the screen where you feel the illusion occurs.
  • After drawing, you will answer the following questions:
    1. Did you feel the presence of an instrument in the video? (Yes / No)
    2. If yes, separately from the drawing, how many instruments did you perceive? (Enter a number)
    3. How interesting or impressive did you find the performance? (Rate on a 7-point scale)
  • The total duration of the experiment, including the tutorial, is approximately 10 minutes.
---

Task Instructions (Crucial)

  • Select a pen color according to the instructions.
  • Play the video (it will start after a 3-2-1 countdown).
  • While watching the video, mark (draw) the area(s) where you feel the presence of an instrument.
    • Use circles, squares, or other shapes to mark the locations.
    • If you do not feel an instrument is present, you do not need to mark anything.
    • The video plays only once. You cannot redo it.
  • After watching and marking (drawing), answer the three questions:
    • Did you feel the presence of an instrument? (Yes / No)
    • If yes, how many instruments did you perceive? (Enter a number)
    • Rate how interesting you find the performance on a 7-point scale.
  • Click the "Next Page" button to proceed. You cannot go back to previous pages.
    • This sequence is repeated for 8 videos.

    A practice page is available before the main experiment. You can freely use it to get familiar with the interface.

    The practice page plays a metronome sound. Please adjust your PC volume so you can hear it clearly, as sound will also be used in the experiment.

    When viewing the video, please be aware that this is a performance using gesture input for musical expression, not simple pantomime, and carefully observe and listen to both the video and the sound.

    Participation Requirements & Notes

    • Participation in this experiment requires that you are 18 years of age or older and that you are making the decision to participate based on your own free will.
    • No prior musical experience is required.
    • Please participate in an environment where you can hear videos with sound (headphones or speakers).
    • Your responses will be collected anonymously and will not include personally identifiable information.
    • Do not share or distribute the videos.
    • This is a psychological experiment and may include illusory elements, but it does not pose any special stress.
    • [Data and Ethics] The data you provide will be used strictly for academic research purposes and nothing else.

    Contribution to Research

    This experiment helps to better understand audience perception of digital instruments. The results contribute to research in music information processing and human-computer interaction (HCI).
